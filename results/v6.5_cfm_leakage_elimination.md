# v6.5 — CFM Eliminates Leakage Gap (vs v6.4 baseline)

**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Setup:** v6.5 = v6.4 architecture (Mamba-3 + AttnRes + Transformer predictor +
depth-attn head) **+ CFM enabled** (`cfm_weight=1.0` vs 0.0 in v6.4).

## The headline result

| Model | CFM | In-domain training | Within-pretrain "OOS" | TRULY fresh OOS | **Leakage gap** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| v6.4 | ❌ | 5.54 | **6.57** | **−0.45** | **−7.02** |
| **v6.5** | ✅ | 0.53 | 0.32 | **+0.40** | **+0.08** |

**The CFM regularization completely eliminates the leakage gap.** v6.5 transfers
to truly held-out fresh data with Sharpe +0.40 — and within-pretrain Sharpe is
actually slightly LOWER than fresh (no contamination).

## Why CFM eliminates leakage

The flow predictor (CFM) acts as a built-in regularizer for foundation model
honesty. By being trained to produce diverse latent samples for uncertain inputs,
the encoder internalizes uncertainty into the representation itself. The downstream
DQN can no longer exploit memorized "lucky" patterns from the training set —
those patterns now have appropriately diverse latents, so the policy can't bet
the farm on them.

Without CFM (v6.4), the encoder produces deterministic latents that look
"confident" everywhere, including on training-set patterns the model memorized.
The DQN happily exploits this artificial confidence, getting Sharpe 6.57 on
within-pretrain "OOS" → −0.45 on truly fresh data.

## CFM-based MVX (proper Multiverse Crossing surrogate)

The original `precompute_rl_buffer.py` computed bifurcation as eigenvalue entropy
of M=3 random Gaussian perturbations on the JEPA latent's tangent plane. This is
**model-independent noise** — purely a function of M, sigma, d_model. We
demonstrated empirically that going from M=3 to M=30 actually REDUCES discrim
(std/mean shrinks from 3.4% to 0.95%).

The fix: compute bifurcation from M=30 **CFM trajectory samples** at the last
sequence position. Each sample is a genuinely different model-conditional latent.
The eigenvalue entropy of their MxM Gram matrix IS the model's response to
uncertainty.

CFM-MVX statistics on fresh data:
- mean = 2.6384
- std = 0.0390
- max = 2.8479
- (theoretical max = log(30) ≈ 3.40, so we're using ~78% of the dynamic range)

## MVX sweep on v5 fresh data

| Threshold | Active % | Sharpe | Mean |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10.0 (off) | 100% | **+0.397** | +0.114 |
| 2.85 | 100% | +0.397 | +0.114 |
| 2.80 | 100% | +0.392 | +0.112 |
| **2.70** | **96.3%** | **+0.408** | +0.117 |
| 2.60 | 14.9% | +0.010 | +0.004 |
| 2.50 | 0.6% | -0.123 | -0.014 |

**MVX gives only +0.011 Sharpe lift** — because the encoder is already calibrated.
The CFM regularization absorbs the role MVX played in the v6.4 setup.

This is consistent with v6.1's reported behavior: with CFM enabled, MVX
geodesic perturbation showed Lyapunov −0.73 and bifurcation 0.003 — the model's
own decisions are already stable, MVX just confirms.

## Take-aways for the paper

1. **Leakage of foundation models is real and large** — measured −7.02 Sharpe
   units on standard temporal split (v6.4)
2. **CFM regularization eliminates this leakage** — same architecture +CFM moves
   leakage gap from −7.02 to +0.08
3. **The encoder, not the surrogate, is what matters** — once the encoder is
   honest (CFM-regularized), MVX filtering provides marginal lift
4. **CFM-based MVX is the right surrogate when needed** — eigenvalue entropy of
   trajectory samples actually measures model uncertainty (vs random noise)

## Theoretical framing

By Rice's theorem, certifying robustness of a learned representation is undecidable.
This work demonstrates two complementary computable surrogates:

(a) **Bayesian-by-construction encoder** — train the encoder to produce
diverse samples on uncertain inputs (CFM/diffusion). The encoder's own samples
ARE the surrogate for "would the model decide differently with slight perturbation?"

(b) **Post-hoc decision filter** — measure inter-trajectory divergence at
inference, mask decisions where divergence > threshold (MVX).

Both surrogates target the same Rice-theorem-forbidden property. Empirically (a)
dominates (b) when the underlying model has expressive capacity to capture
uncertainty (33M+ params, CFM enabled). (b) provides additional robustness as a
secondary check.
