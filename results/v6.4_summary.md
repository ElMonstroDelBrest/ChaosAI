# v6.4 — Architecture Ablation (Mamba-3 + AttnRes + Transformer Predictor)

**Run date:** 2026-04-21 / 2026-04-22
**Compute:** TPU v6e-8 (europe-west4-a)
**Architecture changes vs v6.1:** all 4 features ON
- `encoder_type=mamba3` (trapezoidal disc + complex SSM via RoPE on B/C + BCNorm)
- `use_attn_res=True` (DenseFormer-style attention residuals across encoder depth)
- `use_depth_attn_ret_head=True` (return prediction attends over all layer outputs)
- `predictor_type=transformer` (asymmetric d_pred=64 < d_model=512, full-context cross-attn)

VICReg unchanged from v3 baseline (`var_weight=100`, `cov_weight=10`).

## Bifurcation index (pre-calculated in RL buffer)

- M=3 perturbations on JEPA latent
- Mean = 0.165, Max = 0.195
- v6.1 reference: 0.003 (M=30) — **bifurcation higher in v6.4**

The increased bifurcation suggests v6.4's expanded architecture (Mamba-3 + AttnRes
+ Transformer predictor) creates a more expressive but less smooth representation.
With M=3 (vs M=30 for v6.1) the bifurcation is also overestimated.

Despite higher bifurcation, OOS Sharpe **increased 2.4×** — the architecture
gains overcome the stability cost. Worth re-measuring with M=30 for fair
comparison with v6.1.

## Caveats

1. **OOS slice is biased crypto-heavy** (662 crypto vs 1 stock) due to
   `seq_cutoff_ratio=0.8` correlating with asset listing dates (newer = crypto).
2. The 6.57 Sharpe is for in-distribution market regime — not a true crisis split.
3. Bifurcation comparison v6.4 vs v6.1 needs M=30 for apples-to-apples.
4. Multiverse Crossing 30-universe analysis requires the CFM flow predictor,
   currently disabled in v6.4 (`cfm_weight=0`). Re-enabling CFM and re-training
   would give the geodesic perturbation breakdown.
